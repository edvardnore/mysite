About Me

This is what I do.

In this dissertation I will study what the role of sense-making is amongst fintech entrepreneurs in London. Sense-making’s simplest definition is “the making of sense”, amongst leaders it is the process of how we make sense of the world around us through aligning and understanding different priorities within and outside organisations. Few people are faced with as much uncertainty as entrepreneurs, how past experiences and perception of the future influence their actions and why their product will be essential to it is what I will study, and if a hypothesis can be developed for further study of how they make sense of the world.

Taking part will entail a roughly 30 minute interview and I am looking for fintech entrepreneurs that used to work in finance. This will provide a good sample with a consistent background, and can also provide interesting data on why people left the financial sector after the 2008 financial crisis. This research has gotten strong support from the LSE Department of Management, of which I am currently a student, as no one has looked at entrepreneurs as a group from this angle before and will greatly contribute to our society’s understanding of technology driven innovation in new sectors.

I hope you are excited by this and will consider taking part! If you have any questions do not hesitate to get in touch with me on E.Nore@lse.ac.uk!
